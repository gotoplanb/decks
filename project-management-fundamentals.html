What is project management?

Project management is the discipline of planning, organizing, motivating, and controlling resources to achieve specific goals. A project is a temporary endeavor designed to produce a unique product, service or result with a defined beginning and end (usually time-constrained, and often constrained by funding or deliverables), undertaken to meet unique goals and objectives, typically to bring about beneficial change or added value. (Wikipedia)

tl;dr

Prioritize what you will do to increase project value.

Types

  • Waterfall
  • Kanban
  • Scrum

Waterfall

  • Used for projects that cannot tolerate incomplete featureset and rapid releases are prohibitively expensive.
  • Hardware and software sold in retail packaging.
  • Tools include Jira
  • Common pitfalls are a) poor estiamtes of workforce capacity, b) no mechanism for overcoming delays other than more people.

Kanban

  • Used for projects that span many business units. Tasks tend to be discrete and easy to estimate time. Tolerant of inconsistent work hours.
  • Product launch requires coordination across design, dev, marketing and sales to come together simultaneously. Cross-unit goals are adjustable.
  • Tools include Trello + GitHub issues/milestones
  • Primary pitfall is having cards with wildly varying scope, yielding a board with too little detail to start work or too much detail to visualize trajectory.

Scrum

  • Used for continual work over set time intervals with sole goal of increasing product. Workfroce capacity, team roles and value metrics should be known.
  • Beta product has been released and users are uncovering issues. Each two-week sprint is prioritized to solving issues that either make customer acquisition easier or decrease churn.
  • Tools include PivotalTracker or Sprintly along with sprint retrospective and planning meetings. Daily standups required.
  • The primary pitfall is an inability to break features into small enough tasks that can be finished during a sprint while still increasing value, whether real or perceived by stakeholders. Also, strong-willed stakeholders can ruin a sprint by changing priroities after planning day.
